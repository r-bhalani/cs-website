About

I'm Ruchi, a 3rd year computer science major currently pursuing a concurrent BS and MS at UT Austin with a concentration in ML/AI. I'm currently working as a Teaching Assistant for Behavioral Ethics in the Digital Age and a peer mentor and undergraduate researcher for the Learning Agents Research Group at the AI Lab. Our most recent project was Tail Wagging the Dog: Perceptions of Canine vs. Non-Canine Behavior on a Quadruped Robot, which won the CS Award of Excellence at the 2022 Undergraduate Research Forum.

My skills include Java, C, C++, Python, ROS (Robotics Operating System), Digital Ethics, JavaScript, React, SQL, Flask, ML, and AI.

I spent the summer of 2022 working as a Software Development Engineering Intern at Amazon on the AWS Commerce Platform at their headquarters in Seattle, WA. I will be heading back to the same team for the Summer of 2023.

In my free time, you can find me reading poetry, learning new instruments, or watching movies!

Projects

Spot Making Social Body Language

C++

This project presents a development process to improve upon Boston Dynamics's quadruped robot Spot navigation behaviors to produce behaviors that are more dog-like in appearance.

Winner of 2022 CNS Award for Excellence in Computer Science and Computer Engineering.

Human Body Movement Recognition and Robotic Emulation

Python | ROS

Body imitation by a humanoid simulation using Azure Kinect SDK for body tracking, ROS, and the Darwin-OP. The system can process a video of human body movements, categorize the movements, and then accurately imitate it on a humanoid robot.

Diminishing Disasters

React | SQL Alchemy | Postgre SQL | Python | Postman | Jest | Selenium

Website that connects users to charitable organizations providing relief for natural disasters around the world.

Crime Clearance Status Predictor

ML | Python | Jupyter

Trained on the Austin Crime database entries over the past year and uses multiple different models to try and predict whether a crime will be cleared or not by the police department in the district.

Fine-Tuning SQuAD Against Adversarial Examples

Python | NLP | ML

Making an ELECTRA-small model trained on the large-scale labeled Stanford Question-Answering Dataset robust against an adversarial challenge set produced by Robin Jia and Percy Liang (2017) through fine tuning and inoculation experiments.

UT Austin Pandemic Simulator

Python | RL | AI

Reinforcement learning-based pandemic simulator model that models the UT Austin population of students and staff, as well as university-specific locations such as apartments, campus, and parties.
